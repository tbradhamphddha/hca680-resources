Welcome

Welcome to HCA 680

Welcome to Integrity of Care: Patient Safety and Risk. This course is part of the Master of Healthcare Administration (MHA) program at Edgewood University and is designed to prepare you to lead safe, high-reliability healthcare organizations.

Throughout this 8-week course, you will build the knowledge, analytical skills, and professional identity needed to champion patient safety, manage risk effectively, and foster organizational cultures where every team member feels empowered to speak up for safety.

About This Course

Course Description

This course explores the principles, methodologies, and best practices for developing and sustaining safe healthcare delivery systems that protect patients from harm while promoting organizational excellence. Students will examine comprehensive approaches to patient safety, including risk identification, assessment, and mitigation strategies, through systematic quality improvement methodologies.

Key topics include Failure Mode and Effects Analysis (FMEA), Root Cause Analysis (RCA), National Patient Safety Goals, Communication and Resolution Programs (CRP), Clinical Risk Management (CRM), adverse event disclosure protocols, and the analysis of adverse and sentinel events.

The course emphasizes the development of high-reliability organizational cultures that promote psychological safety, transparent communication, and continuous learning from safety events. Through case studies, simulation exercises, and practical applications, students will develop the competencies necessary to implement evidence-based patient safety initiatives that enhance patient outcomes, reduce preventable harm, improve organizational performance, and foster sustainable safety cultures across healthcare delivery systems.

What You Will Achieve

Course Objectives

  1. Analyze and Design Safe Healthcare Delivery Systems: Evaluate current safety performance using quality indicators and design systematic approaches to reduce medical errors, prevent healthcare-associated harm, and enhance patient safety across the continuum of care.
  2. Implement Evidence-Based Risk Management Strategies: Apply clinical risk management principles, conduct comprehensive risk assessments, and develop proactive mitigation strategies that align with regulatory requirements and organizational values.
  3. Conduct Systematic Safety Event Analysis: Perform root cause analysis and failure mode effects analysis to identify system vulnerabilities, analyze contributing factors, and develop sustainable corrective action plans.
  4. Foster High-Reliability Organizational Culture: Design and implement strategies to create psychologically safe environments that promote error reporting, transparent communication, and continuous learning from safety events.
  5. Lead Communication and Resolution Initiatives: Develop competencies in adverse event disclosure, implement Communication and Resolution Programs (CRP), and demonstrate ethical leadership in patient safety crisis management.
