About This Case Series

This series of 15 cases walks you through the Safety Event Classification (SEC) framework used in healthcare organizations to identify, classify, and respond to patient safety events. Each case presents a clinical vignette and guides you through five discussion points: identifying deviations from Generally Accepted Performance Standards (GAPS), determining whether the deviation reached the patient, scoring the level of harm, determining sentinel event status, and applying the SAC Priority Matrix.

Cases may be completed in any order. Your progress and responses are saved in this session. Use the Download Log buttons to generate a record of your work to submit.

Important: Save Your Work Before Closing This Page

Your responses are stored in your browser session only. If you close this tab, refresh the page, or navigate away, all recorded responses will be permanently lost. Download your response log before closing.

How to Use the Response Log

1
Complete a case. Work through all five discussion points in a case and reach the Final Summary screen. Your responses are recorded automatically when you reach the summary.
2
Download per case. Use the download button in the top-right corner of any open case to save a log for that case only. You can do this immediately after completing each case.
3
Download all cases. Use the Download Complete Response Log button on this page to generate a single log covering every case you have completed in this session.
4
Save as PDF. When the log opens, use your browser's print dialog (File → Print, or Ctrl+P / Cmd+P) and select "Save as PDF." This creates the file you will submit to your instructor.
5
Redoing a case. If you restart a case and complete it again, both attempts are recorded separately in your log. Each attempt shows your selections, the feedback you received, and your total time on that attempt.

The log captures your first selection at each discussion point, the feedback you received, and your total time on the case. Classification answers are only shown in the log after you complete a case so that working through the cases first is required to see results.
